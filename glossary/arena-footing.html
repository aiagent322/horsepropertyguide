Riding Arena Footing

Riding arena footing is the surface material used in a horse arena to provide traction, cushioning, and support for horses during work. Common footing materials include native dirt, decomposed granite, sand, rubber, and engineered blends of sand and fiber or rubber. The right footing depends on the discipline, climate, and drainage characteristics of the arena. In Arizona's dry climate, native decomposed granite is frequently used for outdoor arenas. Poor footing — hard-packed dirt, overly deep sand, or surfaces that become slick when wet — increases the risk of injury to horses and riders. Appraisers assess arena footing condition when evaluating equestrian improvements, and deteriorated footing reduces contributory value.

Key Points

  • Arena footing provides traction and cushioning for horses during work.
  • Common materials include decomposed granite, sand, rubber, and engineered blends.
  • Footing selection depends on discipline, climate, and arena drainage.
  • Deteriorated or inadequate footing reduces the appraised contributory value of an arena.
