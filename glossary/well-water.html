Well Water

Well water refers to groundwater accessed through a drilled or bored well on the property. A private well is the preferred water source for horse properties in rural Arizona because it provides a continuous, on-site supply for both residential and livestock use. Well yield — measured in gallons per minute — determines whether a well can support a horse operation alongside residential demand. Horses require approximately 10 to 15 gallons of water per day, with higher consumption during hot weather. Low-yield wells may be adequate for residential use but insufficient for equestrian use. Lenders and appraisers assess well yield as part of property evaluation. Well registration and pump test documentation should be reviewed by buyers before closing.

Key Points

  • Private wells provide on-site, continuous water and are preferred for rural horse properties.
  • Horses require 10 to 15 gallons per day; well yield must support both residential and equestrian use.
  • Lenders and appraisers evaluate well yield as part of collateral assessment.
  • Buyers should review well registration and pump test records before closing.
