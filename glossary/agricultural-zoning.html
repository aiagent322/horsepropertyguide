Agricultural Zoning

Agricultural zoning is a land use designation that allows farming, ranching, livestock-keeping, and related operations as primary permitted uses. It is the most favorable classification for horse property because it typically imposes no density limits on horses, allows barns and agricultural structures as permitted uses, and supports commercial equestrian operations such as boarding and training without requiring additional permits. In Arizona, agricultural zoning designations include AG, A-1, A-2, and General Agricultural. Parcels with agricultural zoning may not qualify for conventional residential financing, often requiring farm credit or portfolio loan products instead. Agricultural classification also affects property tax — land assessed as agricultural typically carries a lower tax rate than residential land.

Key Points

  • Agricultural zoning is the most permissive classification for horse operations.
  • It typically allows horses, barns, and commercial equestrian use without additional permits.
  • Agricultural-zoned property may not qualify for conventional residential financing.
  • Agricultural classification reduces property tax assessment in most states including Arizona.
