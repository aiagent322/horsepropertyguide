Hauled Water

Hauled water is water transported to a property by truck and stored in above-ground tanks or cisterns for residential and livestock use.

It is a legal and common practice in rural Arizona where private wells are absent, yield is insufficient, or utility water service is unavailable.

Hauled water is typically purchased from municipal fill stations or private suppliers and stored in polyethylene or galvanized tanks sized to meet daily demand. While legal, hauled water has significant limitations for horse property buyers: it introduces ongoing cost and supply dependency, may not satisfy county building permit requirements, and reduces financing eligibility and appraised value compared to properties with private wells or utility water service.

Key Points

  • Hauled water is legally permitted throughout Arizona for livestock and domestic use.
  • It introduces ongoing cost, supply dependency, and operational complexity.
  • Lenders may decline or discount properties relying solely on hauled water.
  • Hauled water typically does not satisfy county building permit water supply requirements.
