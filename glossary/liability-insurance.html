Liability Insurance

Liability insurance for horse property protects the property owner against financial loss from claims of bodily injury or property damage arising from equestrian activities on the premises. Horse-related injuries are among the most common causes of farm and ranch liability claims. Standard homeowners insurance policies typically exclude equine-related liability, meaning owners must purchase a separate equine liability or farm and ranch policy to obtain coverage. Commercial equestrian operations — boarding facilities, training barns, riding schools — require commercial general liability coverage sized to their operation. Liability insurance requirements may be specified in lease agreements, boarding contracts, and conditional use permits. Lenders may require proof of adequate liability coverage as a condition of financing on equestrian properties.

Key Points

  • Standard homeowners insurance typically excludes equine-related liability claims.
  • Horse property owners need a separate equine liability or farm and ranch policy.
  • Commercial equestrian operations require commercial general liability coverage.
  • Lenders and conditional use permits may require proof of liability coverage as a financing or operational condition.
