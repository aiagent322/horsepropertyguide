Mare Motel

A mare motel is an open-sided, covered stall structure common in the American Southwest, particularly Arizona. It typically consists of a row of individual stalls with a shared roof, open fronts, and pipe panel dividers between stalls.

Mare motels are favored in hot, dry climates because they provide shade while allowing air circulation, which reduces heat stress for horses. They are less expensive to construct than fully enclosed barns and are generally easier to permit.

Appraisers treat mare motels as equestrian improvements that contribute value when comparable properties with similar structures exist in the market. The term is regional and may not be recognized in other parts of the country.

Construction costs for mare motels are significantly lower than enclosed barns because they require no walls, insulation, or HVAC systems. A basic mare motel in Arizona can be constructed for $3,000 to $8,000 per stall depending on materials, size, and contractor. This cost efficiency makes them a popular choice for Arizona horse owners who want to maximize stall count within a budget. However, mare motels provide less security and weather protection than enclosed facilities and may not be appropriate for horses that require indoor protection from cold, rain, or extreme heat.

Permit requirements for mare motels vary by jurisdiction and structure type. Open-sided shade structures below a certain square footage may not require a building permit in all Arizona counties. However, larger mare motel structures with permanent footings, electrical connections, or roofing materials that constitute permanent construction typically require permits. Buyers should verify permit status for existing mare motels on any property they are considering, as unpermitted mare motels carry the same compliance risks as unpermitted barns — potential exclusion from appraised value and county enforcement exposure.

Appraisers evaluate mare motels as contributory improvements when comparable sales exist that support their value. In Arizona equestrian markets where mare motels are common, a well-maintained permitted mare motel can add meaningful appraised value, particularly when stall count is a primary buyer criterion. In markets where enclosed barns are more common or mare motels are less familiar, the contributory value may be lower or require specific appraiser commentary to support. Buyers should discuss the local market context for mare motel valuation with their appraiser and agent before purchasing a property where mare motels represent a significant portion of the equestrian improvement value.

Key Points

  • A mare motel is an open-sided, covered stall row common in Arizona and the Southwest.
  • It provides shade and ventilation suited to hot, dry climates.
  • Mare motels are less expensive and simpler to permit than enclosed barns.
  • Appraised value depends on market evidence of comparable properties with similar structures.
