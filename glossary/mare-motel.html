Mare Motel

A mare motel is an open-sided, covered stall structure common in the American Southwest, particularly Arizona. It typically consists of a row of individual stalls with a shared roof, open fronts, and pipe panel dividers between stalls.

Mare motels are favored in hot, dry climates because they provide shade while allowing air circulation, which reduces heat stress for horses. They are less expensive to construct than fully enclosed barns and are generally easier to permit.

Appraisers treat mare motels as equestrian improvements that contribute value when comparable properties with similar structures exist in the market. The term is regional and may not be recognized in other parts of the country.

Key Points

  • A mare motel is an open-sided, covered stall row common in Arizona and the Southwest.
  • It provides shade and ventilation suited to hot, dry climates.
  • Mare motels are less expensive and simpler to permit than enclosed barns.
  • Appraised value depends on market evidence of comparable properties with similar structures.
