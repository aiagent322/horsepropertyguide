Deed Restrictions

Deed restrictions are legally binding limitations on land use written into a property's deed and recorded in the public record.

They run with the land, meaning they apply to every future owner regardless of how the property changes hands.

For horse property buyers, deed restrictions can prohibit horses entirely, limit the number of animals, restrict commercial equestrian activity, or require specific fencing and facility standards. Unlike zoning, which is a government regulation, deed restrictions are private agreements enforceable by neighboring property owners, homeowners associations, or original grantors. A property may be correctly zoned for horse-keeping but rendered unusable for that purpose by deed restrictions that predate the current ownership.

Key Points

  • Deed restrictions are private, legally binding limits recorded in the property deed.
  • They transfer to every future owner and cannot be ignored or overridden by new buyers.
  • Deed restrictions can prohibit horses even when zoning permits them.
  • Buyers must review the deed and title report for restrictions before purchase.
