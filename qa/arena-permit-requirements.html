Can You Build a Riding Arena Without Permits?

Whether a riding arena requires a permit depends on its construction type, size, and location, and the applicable county or municipal code. In most Arizona counties, a completely open, unroofed arena with compacted dirt footing and a perimeter fence does not require a building permit because it is classified as a land improvement rather than a structure. However, setback requirements for fencing and grading may still apply, and some jurisdictions require a grading permit for significant earthwork associated with arena construction. Covered arenas with a permanent roof structure — metal pole barns, wood frame, or tensioned fabric — are universally treated as structures requiring a building permit, setback compliance, and in some cases, a site plan review. The permit requirement for covered arenas is triggered by the permanent attachment of a roof, regardless of whether the sidewalls are enclosed. Electrical work within any arena — lighting, outlets, panel boxes — requires an electrical permit regardless of whether the structure itself triggered a building permit. Buyers purchasing property with an existing covered arena should verify that the structure was permitted and that a final inspection was completed. Unpermitted covered arenas can require retroactive permits, structural upgrades to meet current code, or in extreme cases, demolition orders. Confirming permit history through the county building department before purchase eliminates this risk.

Key Takeaways

  • Open, unroofed arenas with dirt footing typically do not require building permits in Arizona.
  • Covered arenas with permanent roofs require building permits in all Arizona jurisdictions.
  • Electrical work within arenas requires electrical permits regardless of structure type.
  • Unpermitted covered arenas create retroactive compliance risk that affects financing and resale.
