When Do You Need a Permit for Horse Facilities?

Permits for horse facilities fall into two categories: building permits for physical structures and use permits for operational activities. Building permits are required for any enclosed or covered structure — barns, enclosed stalls, covered arenas, hay storage buildings, tack rooms with permanent foundations — in all Arizona counties. Open corrals, fence lines, and dirt arenas generally do not require building permits but may require grading permits if significant earthwork is involved. Electrical, plumbing, and mechanical work within any equestrian facility requires the corresponding trade permit regardless of whether a building permit was also required. Use permits — including conditional use permits — are required when the intended equestrian use is not permitted by right under the parcel's zoning classification. An owner in a rural residential zone who wants to operate a commercial boarding facility will typically need a CUP before beginning operations. Operating a commercial equestrian business without the required use permit is a zoning violation subject to cease and desist orders and fines. Permit requirements also apply to well installation, septic systems, and water storage tanks associated with horse facilities, each governed by separate regulatory agencies. Buyers purchasing property with existing horse facilities should request copies of all building permits and final inspection records from the seller and independently verify through the county building department. Facilities without permit records create compliance risk that cannot be resolved after purchase without potentially significant expense.

Key Takeaways

  • Building permits are required for all enclosed or covered equestrian structures.
  • Open corrals and dirt arenas generally do not require building permits.
  • Commercial equestrian operations not permitted by right require a conditional use permit.
  • Buyers should verify permit records through the county building department before purchase.
