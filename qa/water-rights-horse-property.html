Do You Need Water Rights for Horse Property?

Water rights requirements for horse property depend on the state, the water source, and how water is used on the property. Arizona is an appropriation state governed by the Arizona Department of Water Resources (ADWR), and water rights are a distinct legal entitlement separate from land ownership. For domestic and stock watering purposes — which includes watering horses — Arizona law allows property owners to withdraw groundwater from a private well without a separate water right in most areas outside of Active Management Areas (AMAs). Within the five AMAs — Phoenix, Tucson, Prescott, Pinal, and Santa Cruz — groundwater withdrawal is regulated, and wells must be registered with ADWR. Surface water rights in Arizona — from rivers, streams, or canals — require a water right permit issued by ADWR and are subject to the prior appropriation doctrine. Irrigation water delivered through an irrigation district requires a contractual entitlement with the district, not a separate state water right. Buyers purchasing horse property with existing wells should confirm the well's registration status and pump capacity. Properties relying on canal or irrigation water for stock and pasture use should confirm the water entitlement transfers with the property at closing. Water availability affects carrying capacity, property value, and long-term viability of any equestrian operation.

Key Takeaways

  • Arizona allows groundwater withdrawal for stock watering without a separate water right outside AMAs.
  • Within Arizona's Active Management Areas, wells must be registered with ADWR.
  • Surface water rights require a permit under Arizona's prior appropriation system.
  • Irrigation district water entitlements must be confirmed to transfer with the property at closing.
