How Far Must a Barn Be from Property Lines?

The required distance between a barn and property lines is determined by the setback provisions of the applicable zoning ordinance, and it varies by jurisdiction, zoning classification, and the size of the barn. In most rural and agricultural zones in Arizona, side and rear setbacks for accessory structures including barns range from 10 to 25 feet. Front setbacks — measured from the property line along the road frontage — are typically greater, ranging from 40 to 75 feet depending on the district. Some jurisdictions also require a minimum distance between the barn and the primary residential structure on the same parcel. Setback calculations are measured from the closest point of the structure — including roof overhangs in some codes — to the property line, not from the building foundation. Buyers purchasing property with an existing barn should verify that the structure complies with current setback requirements. Barns built before current setback codes were adopted may have legal nonconforming status, which allows continued use but typically prohibits expansion or rebuilding if the structure is damaged beyond a certain percentage of its value. Buyers planning to construct a new barn should obtain the exact setback dimensions from the county planning department before selecting a building site, as minor misplacements can require costly demolition and reconstruction.

Key Takeaways

  • Side and rear setbacks for barns in Arizona rural zones typically range from 10 to 25 feet.
  • Front setbacks for barns commonly range from 40 to 75 feet depending on the district.
  • Setbacks are measured from the closest structural point, including overhangs in some codes.
  • Existing barns may have legal nonconforming status but cannot typically be expanded beyond code.
