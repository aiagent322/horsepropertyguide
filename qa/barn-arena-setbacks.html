What Are Setbacks for Barns and Arenas?

Setback requirements for barns and arenas are established by the applicable zoning ordinance and specify the minimum distance a structure must be located from property lines, rights-of-way, and other structures on the parcel. Setbacks for equestrian outbuildings vary by jurisdiction and zoning classification, and they are typically different from setbacks applied to the primary residential structure. In Maricopa County's Rural Living districts, barns and accessory structures are commonly required to maintain a front setback of 40 to 50 feet, side setbacks of 10 to 20 feet, and rear setbacks of 10 to 20 feet, though specific requirements depend on the sub-district. Covered arenas and shade structures may be treated as buildings rather than fences, triggering full structural setback requirements. Open arenas with no permanent roofing are often treated differently — as improvements to the land rather than structures — and may not be subject to building setbacks, though some counties impose minimum distances from property lines for arena fencing. In addition to property line setbacks, some jurisdictions require minimum distances between equestrian facilities and residential structures on the same or adjacent parcels. Buyers planning to build or expand equestrian facilities should confirm setback requirements with the county planning department before purchasing land or designing improvements. Nonconforming structures built in violation of setbacks can complicate future financing and resale.

Key Takeaways

  • Setback requirements for barns vary by jurisdiction and zoning classification.
  • Front setbacks for rural equestrian structures commonly range from 40 to 50 feet.
  • Covered arenas may trigger building setback requirements; open arenas may not.
  • Nonconforming structures built in setback violation create financing and resale problems.
